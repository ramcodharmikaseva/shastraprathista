About Sri Sharadambal Temple

Sri Sharadambal Temple is dedicated to Goddess Saraswati, the divine embodiment of knowledge, wisdom, music, arts, and learning. Following the Sringeri Sharada Peetham traditions, the temple serves as a spiritual beacon where devotees seek blessings for academic success, intellectual clarity, and creative inspiration.

Sacred Tradition

Following authentic Sringeri traditions established by Sri Adi Shankaracharya, maintaining ancient Vedic rituals.

Center of Learning

Special focus on education with regular Vedic classes, Sanskrit teaching, and spiritual discourses.

Divine Atmosphere

Peaceful environment ideal for meditation, prayer, and spiritual contemplation amidst natural surroundings.

Temple Architecture

Dravidian Style

Traditional South Indian temple architecture with intricate carvings

Vastu Compliant

Designed according to ancient Vastu Shastra principles

Natural Elements

Integration with natural surroundings and elements

Major Festivals

Navaratri

October

Nine-day festival with special poojas, music concerts, and cultural programs dedicated to Goddess Saraswati.

Saraswati Pooja

January - February

Special worship of Goddess Saraswati during Vasant Panchami, attended by students and artists.

Aksharabhyasam

Daily & Auspicious Days

Initiation ceremony for children to begin their education, seeking blessings from Goddess Saraswati.

Special Sevas & Poojas

  • Abhishekam - Ritual bath with milk, honey, and sacred waters
  • Saraswati Homam - Sacred fire ritual for knowledge and wisdom
  • Pushpa Archana - Floral offering with Vedic chanting
  • Vedic Chanting - Continuous chanting of sacred texts
  • Navaratri Pooja - Special nine-day worship during Navaratri
  • Aksharabhyasam - Initiation ceremony for education
  • Annaprasanam - First rice feeding ceremony for infants
